Electroplating Thickness Calculation – Detailed Notes

==============================================================================
1. Fundamental Electroplating Formula
==============================================================================

Electroplating deposits metal ions as a thin, uniform layer on another surface by passing electric current through an electrolyte. The mass of metal deposited is calculated using Faraday’s Law of Electrolysis:

    Deposited Mass, m = (I × t × M × Efficiency) / (n × F)

Where:
    I         : Current (Amperes, A)
    t         : Time (seconds, s)
    M         : Molar Mass of metal (grams/mole, g/mol)
    Efficiency: Fraction of current producing plating (e.g., 95% = 0.95)
    n         : Valency (number of electrons exchanged per ion)
    F         : Faraday's constant (96485 Coulombs per mole, C/mol)

==============================================================================
2. Thickness Formula
==============================================================================

After determining mass, thickness is computed from the formula:

    Thickness, T = m / (rho × A)
    Thickness in µm = [m / (rho × A)] × 10^4

Where:
    rho : Density of metal (grams/cm^3, g/cm^3)
    A   : Surface area to be plated (cm^2)
    10^4: Converts cm to micrometers (1 cm = 10,000 µm)

==============================================================================
3. Explanation of Terms
==============================================================================

Current (I)     : Amount of electricity passed through the electrolyte.
Time (t)        : Total plating duration in seconds.
Efficiency      : Portion of current resulting in actual metal deposition, typically between 85–98% (enter as a fraction or percentage in calculation).
Molar Mass (M)  : Atomic mass of the element (g/mol), specific for each metal.
Valency (n)     : Number of electrons transferred, corresponding to ion charge (e.g., Au has valency 1 in Au+ plating).
Faraday Constant (F): Relates electric charge to amount of substance deposited (96485 C/mol).
Density (rho)   : Mass per unit volume of metal (g/cm^3).
Area (A)        : Plated item’s total surface (cm^2).

6. Worked Example Calculations
==============================================================================

Example: (All use Efficiency = 0.95, Area = 81 cm^2, Time = 3600 s, Current = 1A, F = 96485)

---- For Gold Plating ----
Molar Mass M = 196.96657 g/mol
Density rho = 19.32 g/cm^3
Valency n = 1

Step 1:
m = (1 × 3600 × 196.96657 × 0.95) / (1 × 96485)
m ≈ 6.992 g

Step 2:
T = [6.992 / (19.32 × 81)] × 10^4
T ≈ 44.7 um

---- For Nickel Plating ----
Molar Mass M = 58.6934 g/mol
Density rho = 8.908 g/cm^3
Valency n = 2

Step 1:
m = (1 × 3600 × 58.6934 × 0.95) / (2 × 96485)
m ≈ 1.038 g

Step 2:
T = [1.038 / (8.908 × 81)] × 10^4
T ≈ 14.4 um

---- For Copper Plating ----
Molar Mass M = 63.546 g/mol
Density rho = 8.96 g/cm^3
Valency n = 2

Step 1:
m = (1 × 3600 × 63.546 × 0.95) / (2 × 96485)
m ≈ 1.125 g

Step 2:
T = [1.125 / (8.96 × 81)] × 10^4
T ≈ 15.5 um

==============================================================================
Unit Conversions & Notes for Milliamps
==============================================================================

- 1 Ampere (A) = 1000 milliamps (mA)
    To use milliamps: Current (A) = Current (mA) / 1000
- 1 minute = 60 seconds, 1 hour = 3600 seconds
- 1 cm^2 = 100 mm^2, 1 m^2 = 10,000 cm^2, 1 inch = 2.54 cm
- 1 cm = 10,000 um, 1 mm = 1000 um, 1 cm = 10 mm
- 1 g = 1000 mg
- Always check & convert current to Amperes before calculation.

Example:
If input is 200 mA, use I = 200/1000 = 0.2 A in formulas above.
